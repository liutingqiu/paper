Self-Referential Spectral Action:
Existence and Nontriviality of Fixed Points

2-Page Summary · Tianqi Liu · Zhengzhou University of Light Industry

Problem

In the standard Connes–Chamseddine framework, the Dirac operator $D_0$ encodes $25+$ Standard Model parameters—all externally given. We ask: can $D$ depend on its own spectral action value?

Setup

Given a spectral triple $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D_0)$, define the spectral action $$S[D] = \mathrm{Tr}\, f(D/\Lambda) + \langle\psi, D\psi\rangle.$$ The self-referential equation is $D = D_0 + \Phi(S[D])$, $\Phi: \mathbb{R} \to \mathrm{End}(\mathcal{H})$.

Scaling ansatz. $\Phi(S) = (\kappa \ln(S/S_0) + \beta) D_0$, where $S_0 = S[D_0]$. Set $\lambda(S)=1+\kappa\ln(S/S_0)+\beta$, so $D=\lambda(S)D_0$. This satisfies all spectral triple axioms for $\lambda>0$.

Heat Kernel Expansion

Under $D=\lambda D_0$, the composite map is $$F(S) = C_4\lambda^{-4} + C_2\lambda^{-2} + C_0 + \lambda E,$$ where $C_4\sim\Lambda^4$, $C_2\sim\Lambda^2$, $C_0\sim\Lambda^0$. Coefficients verified against CCM06: $N=96$ fermion degrees of freedom, $a_0 = N\cdot\mathrm{vol}/(4\pi^2)$, the “$24$” factor confirmed.

Main Results

Theorem 1 (Compression). For $|\kappa|<1/10$, $|\beta|<0.02$, $C_4>0$, the map $F$ maps $I=[0.7S_0,\,1.3S_0]$ into itself with $|F'(S)|<1$ on $I$. By Banach's theorem, a unique fixed point $S^*$ exists.

Theorem 2 (Nontriviality). For $\beta\neq0$, $S^*\neq S_0$. First-order correction: $\displaystyle \frac{S^*-S_0}{S_0} \approx -\frac{4\beta}{1+4\kappa}$. With $\kappa=0.08$, $\beta=0.01$: $S^*\approx 0.97\,S_0$ ($3\%$ correction).

$\mathcal{R}$-Invariance (Core Prediction)

The self-consistent conformal factor $\lambda^*=1+\kappa\ln(S^*/S_0)+\beta$ rescales all SM quantities by powers of $\lambda^*$. The dimensionless ratio

$$\mathcal{R} = \frac{\Lambda_{\mathrm{cc}}\cdot G_N^2}{g_{\mathrm{YM}}^4\cdot v^4}$$ is independent of $\lambda^*$, hence independent of $\kappa$ and $\beta$.

This is the unique robust prediction surviving the scalar bottleneck (one equation $\to$ one constraint).

Honest Limitations

  • Scalar bottleneck: one constraint, cannot predict all 25 SM parameters.
  • $\kappa$ and $\beta$ are free parameters; no physical derivation exists.
  • $\Phi(S)=g(S)D_0$ is a mathematical ansatz, not derived from physical principles.
  • Scheme A (inner fluctuation scaling) also works, with compression condition relaxed by $\sim\Lambda^2$.
